Privacy & data use · Policy 1.0

Know what happens to a voice you contribute.

This page explains the current KP AWAZ recording process in simple language. You choose whether to submit a recording, and each new submission requires your agreement to the current policy.

01

What we collect

KP AWAZ collects the audio you record, the sentence or topic attached to it, its language, technical file details, review status, and a versioned record of your consent. Your account also contains private profile information needed to secure and manage your contributions.

02

Why voice data is collected

Voice recordings help represent pronunciation, accents, vocabulary, and everyday expression in the languages spoken across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Approved recordings may support language and speech technology research and development.

03

How review works

Every new recording starts as Pending and is reviewed by an administrator. A recording may be Approved or Rejected. Submission does not guarantee approval or public release, and Pending or Rejected recordings do not increase your score.

04

How approved recordings may be used

Approved recordings may be prepared for language and speech technology research and development. Approval means a recording passed the platform review; it does not by itself mean the recording has been publicly released.

05

What remains private

Passwords, authentication tokens, private email addresses, account identifiers, consent records, rejection feedback, and storage paths are not part of the public leaderboard or public contribution responses. Your public display name appears only under the existing leaderboard preference and eligibility rules.

06

Requesting withdrawal

You can request withdrawal of a contribution through the supported project contact process. Include the contribution reference shown in My Contributions, but do not send a password, verification code, or authentication token. The project will verify the request and explain what action is available for that recording.

Support contact: to be configured before external deployment.

Existing recordings

Older contributions without a structured consent version and timestamp have legacy consent status unknown. They may remain in the system, but they are not treated as externally release-ready on the basis of structured consent.
