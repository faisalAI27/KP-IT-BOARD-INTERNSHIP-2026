About KP AWAZ · زموږ غږونه

Technology should understand the language people actually speak.

KP AWAZ is a community voice initiative created to improve the representation of Pashto and other local voices in modern language technology. The project begins by collecting real speech from people across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa in a structured, voluntary and reviewable way.

The language-access gap begins with missing voices.

Many digital tools are built around the speech data that is easiest to find. When Pashto accents and local ways of speaking are not represented, voice systems may misunderstand the people they are meant to serve.

Community voice collection helps document real pronunciation, regional accents and everyday expression. It creates a stronger foundation for future research without claiming that the final technology already exists.

A careful foundation

Collection now. Research preparation later.

KP AWAZ separates the work of responsibly collecting speech from the later work of preparing approved data for research and development.

Stage A · Current platform

Collect and organize original recordings

Stage A presents phrases, preserves the original browser-recorded audio, records consent, keeps authenticated ownership, and sends every submission through administrator review.

Stage B · Future work

Prepare approved data for research

Stage B will later process eligible approved data for research, speech-recognition development and related language technologies. That conversion and model work is not part of the current public platform.

Community

Participation matters

A useful collection depends on real people choosing to contribute natural speech, accents and pronunciation from across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa.

Consent

A voluntary process

Consent is required before submission. Recordings stay Pending until reviewed, and submission alone does not increase a contributor’s score.

Trust

Choice continues

Private account information is protected, and contributors can use the supported withdrawal process to request exclusion from future data use.

The long-term goal

Reduce the distance between people and digital systems.

Future language technology should be easier to use for people who communicate most naturally in Pashto and other local languages—not only in English or Urdu.
